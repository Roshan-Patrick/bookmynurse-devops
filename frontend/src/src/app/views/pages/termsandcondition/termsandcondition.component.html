Introduction

Welcome to BookMyNurse. These code of conduct rules and policies outline the responsibilities and expectations for all nurses using our platform. By accessing or engaging with our services, you agree to follow these terms.

1. Organizational Integrity

All decisions taken must align with the vision, mission, and future goals of Book My Nurse. Employees must abide by these policies and operate with discipline.

2. Document Verification

At the time of joining, nurses must present original nursing education certificates and other relevant documents for verification.

3. Professional Conduct

  • No sleeping during duty
  • No watching movies or unnecessary phone use
  • No chatting on duty
  • No unauthorized absenteeism
  • No taking leave for non-medical reasons
  • No theft or unethical behavior
  • No involvement in any criminal activities

Violation will result in strict actions including immediate suspension or permanent dismissal.

4. Confidentiality

Nurses must not share personal, client, patient, or internal company details with anyone outside the organization.

5. Transfers and Shifts

Nurses must work at the assigned location. Transfers may happen with prior notice for valid reasons.

6. Visits to Patient Homes

Bringing family or friends to the patientтАЩs house is strictly prohibited without company approval.

7. Personal Responsibility

Nurses must know their duties and contact the office if problems arise during assignments.

8. Medicine Handling

  • Do not give medicine without a doctor's prescription
  • Do not use Siddha, Ayurveda, or Unani medicine

9. Safety and Emergency

  • Leave the place if unsafe
  • Call 100 (police)
  • Inform the company immediately

10. Disassociation from Company

If a nurse leaves in the middle of an assignment, Book My Nurse is not responsible for their future employment or contact.

11. Misuse of Information

Using internal information for personal benefit, fraud, or private services is serious misconduct and will lead to strict action.

12. Former Employee Conduct

Former nurses must not keep contact with clients or misuse Book My NurseтАЩs name for any personal benefit.

13. Unauthorized Services

Offering services using Book My NurseтАЩs name without permission is illegal and punishable.

14. Duty Ethics

  • Wear gloves and mask during patient care
  • Write daily duty reports
  • Report bedsores to the office immediately
  • Follow doctor and supervisor instructions for treatment

15. Medical Procedures

Procedures like Ryle's Tube Insertion, Catheterization, IV Infusion, and Enema require:

  • DoctorтАЩs prescription
  • Consent from family
  • SupervisorтАЩs approval

16. Insulin and Medication Records

  • Maintain insulin chart
  • Record in-time and out-time daily

17. Understanding Patient Conditions

Nurses must understand patient status (conscious, unconscious, bedridden, etc.) and act with empathy.

18. Universal Precautions

  • Use barrier protection
  • Wear gloves and masks
  • Properly discard contaminated material
  • Clean area after procedures
  • Wash hands and uniforms thoroughly

19. Five Rights of Medication

  • Right Patient
  • Right Time
  • Right Route
  • Right Dose
  • Right Medication

Negligence in this area will be treated as a serious violation.

20. Tips and Gifts

Nurses must not accept tips, gifts, or money from patients or families.

21. Resignation Policy

Nurses must give 3 months written notice before resigning. Otherwise, original certificates may be held and legal action taken.

22. Statutory Benefits

Nurses will be enrolled in ESI and EPF schemes. Required documents must be submitted on time.

23. Uniform and Hygiene

  • Uniform must be worn and kept clean
  • Not wearing a uniform may lead to being marked absent

24. Personal Hygiene Kit

Nurses must carry a hygiene kit. Lack of hygiene is a policy violation.

25. Shift Handover

In case of two shifts, the outgoing nurse must complete reporting and the new nurse must properly take over responsibilities.
